Problem → Solution

Four retail HR problems.
One integrated answer.

Each application targets a specific pain point in retail workforce management — and they share a single source of truth.

Problem 01

Manual shift creation takes 8+ hours per week

Spreadsheet-based scheduling ignores skill depth, fairness, and labor law constraints simultaneously. The result is overpaid over-skilled shifts next to under-covered high-demand slots.

Solution — Shift Optimizer

OR-Tools CP-SAT proves the optimal schedule in <15 seconds

651 binary variables. Hard constraints for labor law and skill coverage. Soft constraints for fairness and preferences. A 2-stage objective ensures compliance before cost. Training pairs are embedded as constraints.

Problem 02

Skill gaps are invisible until a holiday crisis hits

Managers don't know whether their current team can cover next month's demand until they're already short-staffed. Reactive hiring is slow and expensive.

Solution — Workforce Planner

Monte Carlo simulation forecasts coverage 12 months out

10,000 roster simulations per skill × shift combination, modelling absence rates, seasonal demand, and skill distribution. Hit-rate and coverage metrics guide proactive hire/train decisions before gaps appear.

Problem 03

Turnover is noticed only after the resignation letter

The average retail turnover cost is $2,500–$7,000 per employee. Most exits are predictable weeks in advance — but the signals are invisible without a model.

Solution — Retention Optimizer

LightGBM Early Warning System with 10 behavioral features

Shortage stress, shift mismatch, overtime accumulation, seasonal churn factors, and engagement trends combine into a per-employee churn probability. Keyman flags trigger instant intervention prompts.

Problem 04

Training plans live in someone's head — or a forgotten spreadsheet

OJT scheduling is managed informally. Trainers are overloaded, trainees are mis-paired, and no one knows when skill coverage will actually improve.

Solution — Training Planner

Auto-generated OJT roadmaps with capacity analysis

Optimal trainer/trainee pairing, shift-aware scheduling (trainees counted as "off-strength"), capacity analysis that solves the chicken-and-egg dilemma, and export to Shift Optimizer for immediate integration.

Technical Stack

Production-grade tools,
not tutorial libraries

⚙️
Optimization Engine
Google OR-Tools CP-SAT

Used internally at Google for vehicle routing, scheduling, and bin-packing. Applied here to prove the optimal weekly shift assignment across 31+ employees with competing hard and soft constraints — returning a mathematically certified result, not a heuristic approximation.

Constraint Programming · Branch & Bound · Integer Programming
🎲
Probabilistic Simulation
Monte Carlo Roster Simulation

10,000 roster draws per skill × shift band, modelling stochastic absence, seasonal demand, and skill distribution. Outputs hit-rate distributions (not point estimates) that quantify the probability of skill shortfall — enabling decisions under uncertainty.

Stochastic Simulation · NumPy · Confidence Intervals
🧠
Machine Learning
LightGBM Churn Prediction

Gradient-boosted decision trees trained on 300-employee synthetic histories with realistic behavioral patterns: overtime accumulation, shift mismatch trends, seasonal churn multipliers, and chronic stress flags. Ensemble with rule-based fallback ensures graceful degradation.

LightGBM · Binary Classification · Feature Engineering
🔗
Integration Architecture
SSOT-Driven Multi-App Pipeline

A shared data_client.py module manages a single source of truth for employee master data, model parameters, and simulation results. session_state passes SO schedules into stress calculations, stress into churn features, and training plans back into shift optimization — forming a closed feedback loop.

Streamlit session_state · JSON SSOT · Modular Pipeline
