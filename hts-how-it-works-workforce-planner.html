Technical Deep Dive

How Workforce Planner works

A probabilistic simulation engine that answers: "Can my current team cover next month's demand — and which skill will fail first?"

Instead of gut-feel staffing decisions, this app runs 10,000 simulated weeks per skill per shift — modelling absence, demand, and skill distribution — to tell you exactly when and where a coverage crisis will hit, months before it arrives.

The Algorithm

Monte Carlo Roster Simulation

Why Monte Carlo? Because absence is random. Demand is seasonal. Skill distribution is lumpy. A single deterministic calculation would be wrong — MC gives us a probability distribution.

# For each skill × shift combination: hit_count = 0 for _ in range(10_000): present = [e for e in qualified_employees if random() > absence_rate] # stochastic absence adv_count = sum(1 for e in present if skill_level[e] == "advanced") if adv_count >= target_advanced: hit_count += 1 hit_rate = hit_count / 10_000 # → probability of meeting coverage

The result is not "you have 4 Advanced staff" but "there is a 73% probability that enough Advanced staff will be present on any given day." This distinction — point estimate vs. probability — is what makes the forecast actionable.

  1. Employee pool is built per skill × shift

    All employees with Advanced or Basic skill for the target are identified. Shift preference filters which employees are likely to be assigned to each shift band.

  2. 10,000 absence draws are made

    Each employee independently "shows up" with probability (1 − absence_rate). This produces 10,000 possible roster configurations, modelling the full range of real-world uncertainty.

  3. Each roster is tested against demand targets

    For each simulation: does the roster meet the Advanced count target? The Basic count target? Each check is binary — met or not met.

  4. Hit rate = proportion of simulations that met target

    hit_rate = hits / 10,000. This is the probability that coverage will be sufficient on any given day. Target: ≥ 90%. Below 70%: critical risk.

  5. Seasonal adjustment for 12-month forecast

    Demand targets are scaled by monthly seasonality coefficients (FMI 2023 retail). Holiday months require more Advanced staff, which drops the hit rate — even if headcount is unchanged.

  6. Action plan generated from gap analysis

    Skills with hit_rate below threshold trigger hire or train recommendations. Priority is set by severity (gap depth) and urgency (peak month proximity). Output feeds directly into Training Planner.

Outputs

What the dashboard shows

① Coverage Hit Rate Heatmap

Skill × Shift band matrix colored by hit rate. 🟢 ≥90% (safe), 🟡 70–90% (watch), 🔴 <70% (critical). The worst shift band for each skill is the scheduling bottleneck.

② adv_avg — Expected Advanced Count

Expected number of Advanced-skilled employees present per shift (accounting for absence). If adv_avg = 1.3 for Deli Evening, the team is operating at a single point of failure most days.

③ 12-Month Seasonal Risk Calendar

Hit rates projected forward 12 months with seasonal demand scaling. Identifies which months will breach the 90% threshold — giving 3–6 months of lead time to hire or train before the crisis.

④ Individual Resilience Score

Per-employee score combining replaceability (how many others share their skill) and retention probability (from Retention Optimizer). Plots as the Talent Vulnerability Matrix — the upper-left quadrant is the highest-risk zone.

⑤ Action Plan (Hire / Train recommendations)

Prioritized list of actions with skill, severity, count, and priority tier. Feeds directly into Training Planner's auto-plan generation. If shortage is so severe that no trainer exists, a hire recommendation is generated first.

Integration

How WP connects to the rest of the suite

→ Training Planner

Action plan feeds TP auto-generation

WP's hire/train action plan is consumed by Training Planner's "Auto-generate from WP" button — immediately creating trainer/trainee pairs and scheduling OJT without manual input.

→ Shift Optimizer

MC results shape safety score thresholds

The hit rates from WP validate whether SO's safety score thresholds are appropriate. If Deli Evening consistently hits 65% in the forecast, the threshold should be adjusted to trigger earlier warnings.

→ Retention Optimizer

Individual resilience feeds churn risk

Low-resilience employees (low replaceability + high churn risk) appear in the Talent Vulnerability Matrix's red zone — the same data that feeds RO's keyman flag and the HR Manager's flight risk count.

→ Dashboard

mc_result drives all KPIs

The mc_result session object is the primary data source for OHI, EVP, 30-day operation risk, and the HR Manager's 30-day skill gap probability — all derived from the same simulation run.

Why It Matters

What this demonstrates

🎲
Probabilistic Thinking

Most HR tools give point estimates ("you need 3 Deli staff"). This gives probability distributions ("there's a 73% chance you'll have enough"). The difference is actionable risk quantification.

📅
Lead Time Creation

Identifying a November Holiday coverage crisis in August creates 3 months of lead time. The same crisis identified in October creates panic hiring. The simulation converts future problems into current decisions.

🔗
SSOT-Driven Accuracy

Because WP draws from the same master_employees.csv as SO, the simulated rosters reflect real skill distributions — not theoretical ideals. The forecast is grounded in who actually works there.

⚡
Speed at Scale

90,000 simulation draws (9 combinations × 10,000) run in seconds using NumPy vectorized operations. This makes interactive "what-if" analysis practical — change absence rate, see the hit rate change instantly.
