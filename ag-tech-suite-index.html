Problem → Solution

Six farm operations problems.
Seven integrated engines.

Each engine targets a specific constraint in specialty crop operations — and they share a single data pipeline driven by live weather.

Problem 01

Crop planning is done by intuition, not data

Deciding which crops to plant, in which parcels, and when is a combinatorial problem too complex for a spreadsheet. Suboptimal rotations leave revenue on the table and over-commit labor during peak seasons.

Solution — Planting Strategy Optimizer

CP-SAT proves the optimal 24-month planting schedule

Binary variables per parcel × crop × month, with hard constraints for parcel exclusivity and labor capacity, and a revenue-maximizing objective that accounts for seasonal price fluctuations. Results pipe directly into the Lifecycle Manager.

Problem 02

Crop growth is invisible until something goes wrong

Growth stage transitions, water stress, pest risk, and harvest timing are tracked informally or not at all. By the time managers notice a problem, the harvest window has narrowed or quality has dropped.

Solution — Crop Lifecycle Manager

GDD thermal accumulation + water balance model per parcel

Daily growing degree days (GDD) accumulate against crop-specific thresholds. Soil moisture, Kona wind days, and pest history all reduce the effective GDD rate. Stage transitions (vegetative → flowering → harvest) trigger automatic field alerts.

Problem 03

Harvest scheduling is manual and misses bottlenecks

Assigning workers to parcels, scheduling trucks, and sequencing harvests across a multi-crop farm is a combinatorial logistics problem. Manual scheduling leads to idle trucks, over-allocated workers, and missed urgency windows.

Solution — Harvest Logistics Optimizer

CP-SAT interval variables solve job-shop harvest scheduling

Tasks are chunked into 2-hour blocks with no-overlap constraints across workers and trucks. High-urgency parcels (flagged by the Health Predictor) carry large penalty weights. Skill matching, OJT pairing, and equipment constraints are all encoded as hard constraints.

Problem 04

Revenue is unpredictable because weather is unpredictable

Drought, trade winds, and temperature swings all affect yield and labor demand in different ways. Farm budgets based on a single expected-yield forecast are routinely wrong. Owners make investment decisions without knowing the distribution of outcomes.

Solution — Yield & Labor Simulator

2,000 Monte Carlo runs across weather scenarios

Each run samples a weather scenario (normal / drought / Kona wind / heavy rain) based on live Open-Meteo probabilities, applies crop-specific yield response functions, and calculates labor demand. Outputs are revenue P10/P50/P90 distributions — not point estimates.

Problem 05

Quality degradation is noticed at the packing shed, not the field

By the time visual defects appear, the harvest window for premium-grade produce has already passed. Urgent harvests triggered too late mean lower prices, higher waste, and rushed labor allocation.

Solution — Crop Health Predictor

LightGBM predicts quality-degradation risk from 11 field features

Days since rain, temperature, humidity, soil moisture, pest history score, Kona wind exposure, sunshine hours, crop type, area, altitude, and days since harvest combine into a per-parcel risk score. Critical parcels (≥0.70) surface as priority alerts in the Harvest Logistics Optimizer.

Problem 06

Senior farmers' tacit knowledge leaves with them

25+ years of crop-specific decision-making lives in one person's head. When that person retires or is absent, younger workers and seasonal staff make costly mistakes. There is no structured system to transfer skills or assign tasks by competency.

Solution — Knowledge Transfer Planner

Task assignment optimization + auto-generated OJT schedules

Workers are profiled by skill level (none / basic / advanced) per crop task. A mathematical model assigns tasks to the most competent available worker, flags succession risks, and generates OJT pairings — embedding them into the Harvest Logistics Optimizer as co-scheduling constraints.

Engine 07
Smart Irrigation Optimizer
💧

Schedules daily irrigation (in 1-hour blocks, max 4h/parcel/day) across a multi-day forecast horizon. Technical Stack

The right tool
for each constraint

⚙️
Optimization Engine (×3)
Google OR-Tools CP-SAT

Used in three separate engines: planting schedule optimization (24-month binary decision variables), harvest logistics scheduling (interval variables + no-overlap), and irrigation scheduling (integer soil moisture variables). CP-SAT returns mathematically certified solutions — not heuristic approximations — and handles hard constraints natively, which matters when a missed urgency window means crop loss.

Constraint Programming · Interval Variables · Integer Programming
🎲
Probabilistic Simulation
Monte Carlo Weather Simulation

2,000 trials per simulation run, each sampling a weather scenario from live Open-Meteo probabilities (Waimea / Hilo / Kona weather stations), then applying Gaussian noise to rainfall, temperature, humidity, and pest risk parameters. Output is a revenue distribution — P10/P50/P90 — that reflects the actual uncertainty of Hawaii's variable weather, not a single optimistic forecast.

Stochastic Simulation · NumPy · Open-Meteo API
🧠
Machine Learning
LightGBM Crop Health Model

Gradient-boosted decision trees trained on 5,000 synthetic records with Hawaii-specific features: Kona wind exposure days, altitude (Mauna Kea slope vs. Kona coast), and crop-type encoding for papaya, coffee, taro, macadamia, and pineapple. Risk scores (0–1) feed urgency weights directly into the Harvest Logistics Optimizer — closing the loop between prediction and scheduling.

LightGBM · Binary Classification · Domain Features
🌦️
Live Weather Integration
Open-Meteo API — Real-Time Data Pipeline

weather_fetcher.py calls the Open-Meteo API to derive real-time scenario probabilities (normal / drought / Kona wind / heavy rain) from current conditions at Hawaii weather stations. These probabilities flow into the Yield Simulator, the Knowledge Transfer Planner (task adjustments by scenario), and the Portal dashboard — making every simulation reflect today's actual conditions, not static assumptions.

Open-Meteo · Real-Time · Scenario Derivation

Multi-Engine Data Pipeline

Seven engines connected through session_state: planting decisions seed lifecycle tracking; health scores set harvest urgency weights; knowledge transfer assignments inject OJT constraints into logistics scheduling. Each engine is independent, but together they form a closed feedback loop.

Product thinking applied to agricultural decision support.
