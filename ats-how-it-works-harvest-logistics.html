Engine 03 — Technical Deep Dive

How the Harvest Logistics Optimizer works

A plain-English explanation of CP-SAT interval variable scheduling — assigning workers, trucks, and equipment across parcels over a multi-day harvest horizon.

Instead of a daily whiteboard assignment, this engine solves the entire week's harvest logistics as a job-shop scheduling problem — guaranteeing that urgent parcels are harvested first, skilled workers are matched to the right crops, and trucks never idle when produce is ready.

The Technology

Job-shop scheduling with interval variables

Interval variables — the key abstraction

CP-SAT Interval Variables + AddNoOverlap

Each task assignment is modelled as an Interval Variable: a start time, duration, and end time — all linked by CP-SAT constraints. AddNoOverlap across all intervals assigned to the same worker (or truck) guarantees no double-booking. This is the same technique used in industrial scheduling for manufacturing lines and airport gates. The solver explores millions of feasible assignment combinations and proves the optimal assignment within the time limit.

Urgency-weighted objective with labor cost

Multi-Priority Objective Function

The solver minimizes: PENALTY_URGENCY_HIGH_MISS × 50,000 + PENALTY_URGENCY_MED_MISS × 10,000 + labor_cost × 1 + truck_cost × 1 − BONUS_ADVANCED_MATCH × 300 − BONUS_FULLTIME_UTIL × 50. A single missed critical harvest (50,000 pts) outweighs hundreds of dollars of extra labor cost. The hierarchy ensures health-flagged parcels are always harvested first, then cost is minimized within that constraint.

Constraint Design

Hard constraints that model real farm operations

HARD

Worker no-overlap

No worker can be assigned two tasks that overlap in time. Implemented via AddNoOverlap over all Interval Variables assigned to each worker. The solver never double-books.

HARD

Truck no-overlap

Each truck can only run one delivery at a time. Interval Variables for truck assignments are subject to AddNoOverlap — a truck carrying papaya to Honolulu Port cannot simultaneously be assigned a macadamia run.

HARD

Worker availability

Each worker has available_days[] per week (accounting for days off, illness, contract limits). Tasks are only assigned on days the worker is available. Seasonal workers have limited availability days.

HARD

Skill matching

Harvest tasks require workers with at least "basic" skill for that crop type. Advanced workers have a higher effective harvest rate (100% vs 70% for basic). Tasks are only assigned to skill-eligible workers.

PRIORITY

2-hour task chunking

Large harvest tasks are split into 2-hour sub-tasks. This maximizes scheduling flexibility — a 6-hour harvest becomes three 2-hour chunks that can be distributed across workers and time slots. Smaller blocks fit more easily into gaps in the schedule.

PRIORITY

OJT pair co-scheduling

When the Knowledge Transfer Planner outputs OJT pairs (senior + junior), they are embedded as soft constraints: the solver prefers to assign both to the same task on the same day. OJT tasks are typed as "ojt" and handled separately from production harvest tasks.

The Math

Objective function & harvest rate model

# Objective (minimize) PENALTY_URGENCY_HIGH_MISS × 50,000 (per missed critical task) + PENALTY_URGENCY_MED_MISS × 10,000 (per missed high-urgency task) + labor_cost_weight × 1 (total daily wage × days worked) + truck_cost_weight × 1 (truck runs × cost_per_run) − BONUS_ADVANCED_MATCH × 300 (per advanced-skill worker on matching crop) − BONUS_FULLTIME_UTIL × 50 (per full-time worker utilized) # Harvest rate model (kg/hour) effective_rate = base_rate[crop] × 0.85 (field efficiency factor) base_rate: papaya=60, coffee=20, taro=80, macadamia=40, pineapple=50 kg/h # Hours needed for a harvest task required_hours = ceil(est_kg / (Σ worker_rate × skill_factor)) skill_factor: advanced=1.0, basic=0.7 # Sub-task chunking while remaining_hours > 0: chunk_duration = min(2, remaining_hours) # 2h max blocks create_subtask(duration=chunk_duration) remaining_hours -= chunk_duration

Why It Matters

What this demonstrates

⚙️
Industrial-Grade Scheduling

Interval variable job-shop scheduling with AddNoOverlap is the same technique used in factory floor scheduling, airport gate assignment, and surgical theater planning. Applied here to a farm, it demonstrates transferable optimization expertise.

🔗
Cross-Engine Inputs

Urgency weights come from the Crop Health Predictor. OJT pairs come from the Knowledge Transfer Planner. Worker availability comes from master_data.py. This engine consumes outputs from three other engines — demonstrating pipeline integration depth.

🌾
Domain Constraints Encoded

Harvest rates per crop type, ethylene cargo incompatibility, Hawaii's early-morning work schedule (6:00–15:00 to avoid afternoon heat), refrigeration requirements — all encoded as first-class model parameters.

📊
Counterfactual Scenarios

The short-staffed scenario button isn't a demo feature — it's a decision support tool. Managers can quantify exactly how much revenue risk they carry if seasonal workers aren't hired, before making the staffing decision.
