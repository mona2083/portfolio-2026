Engine 07 — Technical Deep Dive

How the Smart Irrigation Optimizer works

A plain-English explanation of CP-SAT integer variable optimization — scheduling daily irrigation hours per parcel across a 7-day forecast horizon to minimize water cost while preventing crop stress and respecting Kona wind evaporation dynamics.

Instead of fixed irrigation schedules, this engine treats each parcel's daily watering hours as integer decision variables and solves for the minimum-cost schedule that keeps every parcel's soil moisture above its stress threshold — automatically cancelling irrigation on rainy days, doubling evaporation on Kona wind days, and cutting water to harvested parcels at precisely the right time.

The Technology

CP-SAT integer variable optimization with state propagation

Integer moisture state — 10× scaling trick

Soil Moisture as Constrained Integer Variable

CP-SAT works with integers only. Soil moisture (0–100%) is scaled ×10 to become an integer in range [50, 1000] — allowing 0.1% precision without floating-point arithmetic. Each day's moisture is a chained state variable: M[p, d] = clamp(M[p, d-1] + I[p,d]×150 + rain_gain − evap, 50, 1000). One irrigation hour adds +15% (150 in scaled units), 1mm rain adds +0.5%, and normal evaporation subtracts −4% per day. On Kona wind days (wind direction 160°–240°), evaporation doubles to −8% per day — capturing the desiccating effect of Hawaii's offshore wind system.

Penalty-based minimization objective

Three-Term Cost Function

The solver minimizes: Σ [I[p,d] × water_cost_per_h×10] + Σ [dry_penalty[p,d] × 50] + Σ [pest_penalty[p,d] × 20]. Water cost is proportional to scheduled hours. Dry penalty = max(0, dry_threshold − M[p,d]) — activated when moisture drops below the crop's stress threshold (e.g., papaya dry threshold = 35%). Pest penalty = max(0, M[p,d] − 700) — only for parcels with pest_history_score > 0.4, penalizing over-watering above 70% moisture that promotes fungal disease. The ×50 / ×20 weights ensure preventing crop death (dry penalty) vastly outweighs water cost — correctly prioritizing plant health over efficiency.

Constraints & Logic

The 6 scheduling rules

Hard Constraint

Rain cancellation

If precipitation ≥ 10 mm on a given day (configurable rain_cancel_threshold), irrigation is forced to 0 for all parcels on that day. model.Add(I[p,d] == 0) — no water is wasted during adequate rainfall.

Hard Constraint

Daily total-hour cap

Σ I[p,d] ≤ max_daily_hours across all parcels per day — enforces the farm's water intake infrastructure limit (pump capacity, pipe flow rate). Default 24 hours/day total across all parcels.

Hard Constraint

Pre-harvest water cutoff

Parcels in "harvest" growth stage have irrigation forced to 0. This models the agronomic practice of stopping irrigation 3–7 days before harvest to concentrate sugars and firm fruit — critical for papaya and pineapple quality grades.

Hard Constraint

Post-harvest cutoff (cyclical crops)

After harvest day, cyclical crops (taro, papaya, pineapple) receive no irrigation — the parcel is fallow. Perennial crops (coffee, macadamia) continue receiving irrigation after harvest since the trees are permanent and require ongoing moisture.

Soft Constraint (Penalty)

Dry stress penalty

dry_pen[p,d] = max(0, dry_threshold − M[p,d]). Only applied when water_needed is true (not in cutoff period). The ×50 weight makes crop death prevention the dominant driver — the solver will always prefer spending money on water over incurring this penalty.

Soft Constraint (Penalty)

Over-moisture pest penalty

pest_pen[p,d] = max(0, M[p,d] − 700). Only applied to parcels where pest_history_score > 0.4. Prevents over-irrigation that would push moisture above 70% on already pest-stressed parcels where Phytophthora and other water-borne pathogens thrive.

Moisture state transition & objective

# Variables (per parcel p, per day d): I[p,d] ∈ {0,1,2,3,4} # irrigation hours (integer) M[p,d] ∈ [50, 1000] # soil moisture ×10 (integer) # State transition: evap = 80 if kona_wind else 40 # 8% or 4% per day (scaled) rain_gain = int(rain_mm × 5) # 1mm = +0.5% moisture M[p,d] = clamp(M[p,d-1] + I[p,d]×150 + rain_gain − evap, 50, 1000) # Penalty variables: dry_pen[p,d] = max(0, dry_threshold − M[p,d]) # if water_needed pest_pen[p,d] = max(0, M[p,d] − 700) # if pest_score > 0.4 # Objective (minimize): Σ I[p,d] × cost_per_h×10 + Σ dry_pen[p,d] × 50 # death prevention >> water cost + Σ pest_pen[p,d] × 20 # over-water penalty for pest parcels

Kona wind detection: Wind direction 160°–240° (SE offshore) is flagged as Kona wind, doubling the evaporation rate. This is unique to Hawaii's leeward coast geography — dry offshore air from the Mauna Loa rain shadow evaporates topsoil twice as fast as trade wind days.

Data Pipeline

How data flows through the irrigation optimizer

  1. Parcel sensor data from Crop Health Predictor

    The same 8 parcel profiles used by the Crop Health Predictor (P01–P08) are shared via session_state — including soil_moisture_pct, pest_history_score, growth stage, and crop type. The irrigation optimizer reads these as initial conditions for day 0 of the planning horizon.

  2. 7-day weather forecast injection

    The weather forecast is a list of 7 daily dictionaries: {precip, wind_dir, temp_c, humidity}. In the demo, users can load Open-Meteo API forecasts for any of the 3 Hawaii stations (Waimea/Hilo/Kona) or manually set per-day conditions. Wind direction is decoded to detect Kona wind days (160°–240°).

  3. CP-SAT model construction

    For each (parcel, day) pair, the model creates: I[p,d] integer variable (0–4), M[p,d] integer variable (50–1000), dry_pen and pest_pen integer penalty variables. State transition constraints link each day's moisture to the previous day, irrigation input, rainfall, and evaporation. All constraints are added before calling solver.Solve().

  4. CP-SAT solve (≤ 10 seconds)

    solver.parameters.max_time_in_seconds = 10.0. For a typical 8-parcel × 7-day problem (56 irrigation variables + 56 moisture state variables + penalty variables), CP-SAT finds the optimal or near-optimal solution in under 1 second — the 10-second limit is a safety net for edge cases with unusual weather patterns.

  5. Schedule & moisture trajectory output

    The solution is formatted as two arrays: schedule (per-parcel, per-day irrigation hours and resulting moisture), and trajectories (per-parcel 7-day moisture curve). Both are written to session_state and displayed as an interactive Gantt-style chart on the Field Staff dashboard — showing exactly when each parcel gets watered and what moisture level results.

Days where irrigation was automatically cancelled due to forecast rain ≥ 10mm are flagged in the schedule — showing the water savings attributed to weather-responsive scheduling vs. a fixed weekly program.

Days where Kona wind is detected (wind_dir 160°–240°) are highlighted in the schedule, explaining why extra irrigation hours were assigned — connecting the schedule decision to its meteorological cause.

Why It Matters

The farm management problem it solves

Water is scarce and expensive in Hawaii

Hawaii agricultural water costs $15+/hour for pump operation. On a 21.5-ha farm with 8 parcels, over-irrigation by just 1 hour per parcel per day adds up to $840/week in unnecessary cost. The optimizer finds the minimum-cost schedule that still prevents crop stress — a direct operational saving.

Kona wind is a uniquely Hawaiian challenge

On the leeward (Kona) side of Hawaii's Big Island, SE offshore winds in the Mauna Loa rain shadow create dry conditions that evaporate topsoil at double the normal rate. Fixed irrigation schedules set for normal trade wind days leave coffee and macadamia orchards stressed on Kona wind days — the optimizer adjusts automatically.

Pre-harvest cutoff timing for quality grades

Hawaii's premium papaya and pineapple buyers (Whole Foods, premium hotel chains) require specific Brix sugar levels that only develop when irrigation is stopped at exactly the right pre-harvest window. Manual timing is inconsistent; the optimizer integrates crop stage data from the Crop Lifecycle Manager to enforce precise cutoffs.

Pest-aware moisture ceiling

Taro wetland parcels with high pest history are particularly vulnerable to Phytophthora root rot when over-irrigated. The pest_penalty constraint gives the optimizer a second objective beyond just preventing drought — also preventing the over-wet conditions that the Crop Health Predictor's pest_history_score identifies as fungal disease triggers.
