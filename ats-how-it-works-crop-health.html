Engine 05 — Technical Deep Dive

How the Crop Health & Quality Predictor works

A plain-English explanation of LightGBM binary classification — predicting quality-degradation risk from 11 sensor features and triggering harvest-priority alerts before produce value is lost.

Instead of waiting until a field worker notices visible degradation, this engine continuously scores every parcel from sensor readings using a LightGBM model trained on 5,000 synthetic records — generating a 0–100% degradation probability that directly drives the urgency level fed into the Harvest Logistics Optimizer.

The Technology

LightGBM gradient-boosted binary classification

LightGBM — leaf-wise tree growth

Gradient Boosting on 11 Agricultural Features

LightGBM builds an ensemble of 200 decision trees (num_boost_round=200) using leaf-wise growth, which outperforms level-wise XGBoost on tabular data by focusing splits on the leaves with the highest loss reduction. Key hyperparameters: max_depth=5, num_leaves=31, learning_rate=0.05, feature_fraction=0.8, bagging_fraction=0.8 (bagging_freq=5). Binary log-loss objective with sigmoid output produces a calibrated probability in [0, 1]. Feature importance by gain identifies which sensor readings matter most — pest_history_score, days_since_harvest, and soil_moisture_pct are consistently the top three drivers.

Synthetic training data with rule-based labels

5,000-record Synthetic Dataset

Since real sensor logs are unavailable for the demo, the model trains on 5,000 synthetic records generated by a rule-based risk function: drought stress (days_since_rain > 10 → +0.2 risk), heat stress (temp > 30°C → +0.15), dry soil (soil_moisture < 25% → +0.20), high humidity + Kona winds (humidity > 75% & kona_days > 5 → +0.30), pest history (+0.25 × pest_score), and over-maturity (days_since_harvest > crop-specific threshold → +0.35). Gaussian noise (σ=0.15) is added before binarisation — teaching the model to identify degradation patterns without overfitting to exact thresholds.

The 11 Features

Sensor & metadata inputs to the model

days_since_rain
Days elapsed since last measurable rainfall — drought proxy. Above 10 days begins triggering drought stress contribution.
avg_temp_c
7-day rolling average temperature (°C). Heat stress activates above 30°C; contributes up to +0.15 risk score.
avg_humidity_pct
7-day rolling average humidity (%). Critical only when combined with Kona wind days — high humidity + wind interaction drives fungal and pest risk.
soil_moisture_pct
Real-time soil moisture sensor value (%). Below 25% triggers dry-soil degradation risk (+0.20). Shared with Smart Irrigation engine.
days_since_harvest
Days since last harvest — maturity proxy. Each crop has its own over-maturity threshold: papaya=25d, coffee=40d, taro=45d, macadamia=50d, pineapple=35d.
pest_history_score
90-day accumulated pest incidence score (0–1). Directly contributes +0.25 × score to risk. Also used by Smart Irrigation to modulate over-moisture penalty.
kona_wind_days_30d
Number of Kona wind days (SE offshore wind) in the last 30 days. Interacts multiplicatively with high humidity to increase fungal risk.
sunshine_hours_7d
Total sunshine hours over the past 7 days. Below 4 hours contributes +0.10 risk — insufficient photosynthesis affects fruit sugar content and shelf life.
crop_type_enc
Label-encoded crop type (papaya=0, coffee=1, taro=2, macadamia=3, pineapple=4). Allows the model to learn crop-specific degradation patterns implicitly.
area_ha
Parcel area in hectares (0.3–5.0 in demo data). Larger parcels have more micro-climate variation; the model learns to slightly discount risk on larger parcels.
altitude_m
Parcel altitude (50–800m). Kona-side upland coffee parcels (550m+) experience cooler temperatures and different pest profiles than coastal papaya fields.

Model pipeline

# Training (first run — ~2 seconds): df = generate_training_data(n_samples=5000, seed=42) # Rule-based risk score → Gaussian noise → binarise at 0.5 X = df[FEATURES] # 11 columns y = df["is_degraded"] # 0 or 1 params = {"objective":"binary","max_depth":5,"num_leaves":31, "learning_rate":0.05,"feature_fraction":0.8,"bagging_fraction":0.8} model = lgb.train(params, lgb.Dataset(X, y), num_boost_round=200) model.save_model("lgbm_model.txt") # Inference (every subsequent run — milliseconds): model = lgb.Booster(model_file="lgbm_model.txt") prob = model.predict(parcel_features_df)[0] # → 0.0–1.0 level = "critical" if prob≥0.70 else "high" if prob≥0.50 else "medium" if prob≥0.30 else "low"

Data Pipeline

How data flows through the predictor

  1. Sensor data collection from parcel profiles

    The 8 default parcel records (P01–P08) carry pre-populated sensor readings — soil moisture, temperature, humidity, days since rain, pest history, and Kona wind count. In a live deployment these would stream from IoT sensors; in the demo they are editable via sliders.

  2. Model load-or-train decision

    On session start, CropHealthPredictor.load_or_train() checks for lgbm_model.txt. If found, the 200-tree ensemble loads in milliseconds. If not found (first run or deleted cache), the synthetic training pipeline runs, generates 5,000 records, trains the model, and saves it — taking about 2 seconds.

  3. Batch prediction across all parcels

    predict_batch() assembles all parcel sensor readings into a single DataFrame and calls model.predict() once — returning a list of 8 probabilities in one LightGBM forward pass. This is faster than 8 individual calls and enables side-by-side parcel comparison on the dashboard.

  4. Risk level classification & alert generation

    Each probability is mapped to a risk level via the threshold table. generate_alert_message() produces a bilingual (EN/JA) alert string with the parcel name, crop, probability percentage, and action recommendation — displayed as a prioritized alert list on the Field Manager dashboard.

  5. Risk scores exported to Harvest Logistics Optimizer

    Critical and high-risk parcel IDs are written to session_state as harvest_priority_parcels. The Harvest Logistics Optimizer reads this list and assigns urgency_level=3 or 2 to those tasks — ensuring the CP-SAT solver applies the 50,000 or 10,000-point penalty to any schedule that misses them.

Results

What the predictor produces

Per-parcel degradation probability (0–100%)

A sortable table showing all 8 parcels ranked by risk — field staff immediately see which parcel needs attention today, which can wait until Thursday, and which is in nominal condition.

Bilingual prioritized alert messages

Four-level alerts (🔴🟠🟡🟢) with actionable text in both English and Japanese — designed for a mixed field staff where some workers prefer Japanese instructions.

Feature importance breakdown

feature_importance(importance_type="gain") reveals which inputs most influenced the model's predictions — helping the farm manager understand which sensors to invest in and which field conditions most consistently predict quality loss.

Urgency levels fed to Harvest Logistics Optimizer

The 4-level urgency system (critical=3, high=2, medium=1, low=0) is directly consumed by the CP-SAT harvest scheduler — ensuring the math-based optimization always respects biological urgency over cost minimization.

Why It Matters

The farm management problem it solves

From visual inspection to data-driven triage

A field worker can inspect 3–5 parcels per morning walk. A batch prediction scores all 8 parcels in milliseconds — surfacing hidden risk in parcels that look fine visually but have accumulated stress in soil moisture, pest history, and weather data that a human eye cannot integrate.

Revenue protection through timely harvest

Hawaii papaya at 28% degradation risk on a Wednesday is fine. At 72% on a Saturday it's a total loss if not harvested. The threshold-based alert system creates a clear "act now / monitor / normal" decision framework that prevents preventable losses.

Hawaii-specific degradation drivers

Kona wind (SE offshore wind bringing high humidity from Mauna Loa's leeward side) is a uniquely Hawaiian degradation driver that generic crop models ignore. The feature engineering explicitly captures the humidity × kona_wind_days interaction that triggers fungal and pest surge in taro and coffee.

Bridge between sensing and scheduling

Without a quantified risk score, the Harvest Logistics Optimizer has no way to distinguish "this parcel can wait a day" from "this parcel must be harvested today." The predictor creates that signal — turning biological urgency into a numerical input that the CP-SAT solver can optimize against.
