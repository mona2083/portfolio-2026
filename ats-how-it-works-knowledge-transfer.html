Engine 06 — Technical Deep Dive

How the Succession & Knowledge Transfer Planner works

A plain-English explanation of greedy priority-based task optimization — matching veteran farmer tacit knowledge to the right worker-task-day combination, generating OJT pairs, and stress-testing knowledge concentration risk with Monte Carlo simulation.

Hawaii's aging farming workforce means critical tacit knowledge — when to pick Kona coffee by smell, how to identify taro leaf blight before it spreads — is concentrated in one or two senior workers. This engine maps that concentration, simulates what happens if a key person is absent, and auto-generates OJT pairings that force knowledge transfer before the skill walks out the door permanently.

The Technology

Greedy priority assignment + Monte Carlo stress testing

Priority-based greedy assignment

Urgency-weighted task scoring without OR-Tools

Each task receives a priority score: base = URGENCY_WEIGHT[category] × complexity. Category weights: pest_control=4, ojt=6 (intentionally overweighted to protect senior time for knowledge transfer), harvest=3, pruning=2. Weather scenario multipliers apply on top — winter_storm × 2.5 on pest_control, flash_flood × 2.0 on harvest and pest tasks. Monte Carlo-flagged OJT tasks receive +10 bonus, making them the highest-priority items in the queue. Tasks are then greedily assigned in priority order to eligible workers who have the required skill level and remaining hours — no backtracking.

Monte Carlo knowledge stress test

200-trial Absence Simulation

run_knowledge_stress_test() runs the full assignment algorithm 200 times, each time randomly applying absence events: 5% probability a worker is completely absent for the week (turnover/long leave), 15% probability a full-time worker takes 1–2 PTO days, 5% probability of a sick day. For each trial, the set of unassigned tasks is recorded. After 200 trials, the engine computes: per-skill failure_prob (how often tasks in that crop domain go unassigned), per-worker dependency_score (how many extra tasks fail when that person is absent vs. baseline), and key_person_risk ranking. This output drives the urgent OJT generation.

Knowledge Map

Skill concentration risk per crop domain

generate_knowledge_map() scans all 7 workers and classifies each crop domain by risk — based on how many senior workers hold "advanced" skill in that crop.

Crop Domain Risk Level Condition
Any domainCritical0 senior workers hold "advanced" skill — knowledge is completely absent
Any domainHighExactly 1 senior holds "advanced" — single point of failure (key person risk)
Any domainMediumMultiple seniors but total "advanced" workers ≤ 2 — fragile coverage
Any domainLow3+ workers with "advanced" skill — redundant coverage, resilient to absence

Task priority calculation

# Urgency weights by category: URGENCY_WEIGHT = {"pest_control": 4, "ojt": 6, "harvest": 3, "pruning": 2} # Base priority score: base = URGENCY_WEIGHT[category] × complexity # complexity ∈ {1,2,3} # OJT bonus tier: if mc_priority: base += 10 # MC-flagged urgent OJT → top of queue else: base -= 2 # Rule-based OJT → slightly lower # Weather multipliers: if winter_storm and pest_control: base *= 2.5 if flash_flood and (harvest or pest_control): base *= 2.0 # Eligibility check before assignment: can_assign = (skill_level[worker][crop] >= required_skill[task]) and (not senior_required or worker.is_senior) and (worker_hours_remaining >= task.duration_hours)

OJT Generation

Two-tier automatic OJT pairing

Tier 1 — Monte Carlo-driven (Priority +10)

Urgent OJT from Stress Test Results

For every skill domain where the stress test shows failure_prob ≥ 10%, an urgent OJT task is auto-generated (OJT_MC_CROPNAME). The system finds the senior worker who holds "advanced" skill in that crop and pairs them with the highest-priority junior worker who doesn't. These tasks receive priority +10, making them the absolute highest priority in the weekly schedule — the algorithm forces senior time allocation for critical knowledge transfer before scheduling any other work.

Tier 2 — Rule-based (Normal Priority)

Knowledge Map-Driven OJT

Independently, generate_knowledge_map() identifies crop domains with "critical", "high", or "medium" concentration risk and generates OJT_RB_CROPNAME tasks for each. These have slightly lower priority (base − 2) than Monte Carlo-flagged tasks but still rank above most routine farm work. Both tiers enforce the same pairing logic: exactly one senior trainer (advanced skill in that crop) + one junior trainee, 3-hour blocks.

Data Pipeline

How data flows through the planner

  1. Worker profiles & task catalog loaded from master_data

    get_default_workers() provides 7 worker profiles with skill maps (crop → none/basic/advanced), roles, availability, and daily wage. get_default_tasks() provides the farm task catalog with complexity, duration, and crop assignment. Both are loaded once and cached.

  2. Monte Carlo stress test generates task_failure_probs

    run_knowledge_stress_test() runs 200 random absence simulations. The output task_failure_probs list — sorted by failure probability descending — identifies which crop skill domains are most vulnerable. This is the primary input for Tier 1 OJT generation.

  3. OJT tasks dynamically injected into the task queue

    optimize_task_assignment() receives stress_result as an optional argument. If provided, it first generates Tier 1 MC-driven OJT tasks, then appends Tier 2 rule-based OJT tasks from generate_knowledge_map(). Both tiers are merged into the task list before priority sorting.

  4. Priority sort & greedy assignment across 7 days

    All tasks (regular + OJT) are sorted by priority score descending. For OJT tasks, a senior+junior pair is forced — if no eligible trainer or trainee is available with sufficient hours, the task goes to the unassigned list. For regular tasks, the first N eligible workers with remaining hours are selected. worker_hours_remaining tracks remaining capacity per worker.

  5. KPI output & session_state export

    KPIs include: coverage_pct (assigned/total tasks %), ojt_sessions completed, knowledge_transfer_score (ojt_sessions / needed_ojt), and avg_senior_utilization. Results are written to session_state for the Farm Owner dashboard — showing how well tacit knowledge is being protected this week alongside revenue metrics.

Results

What the planner produces

Weekly task assignment schedule

A day-by-day table showing which worker handles which task — including OJT sessions with trainer/trainee pair names, crop, and hours. Field staff see their weekly schedule; managers see coverage gaps.

Knowledge concentration risk map

A per-crop risk table (Critical / High / Medium / Low) showing which crops have single-point-of-failure senior dependency — the primary input for board-level succession planning discussions.

Key person dependency ranking

The Monte Carlo stress test produces a ranked list of workers by dependency_score — measuring how many additional tasks fail when each person is absent vs. the baseline absence rate. High scores identify who the farm cannot afford to lose.

Knowledge transfer score (0–100%)

min(ojt_sessions_completed / ojt_sessions_needed, 1.0) × 100 — a single number that tells the Farm Owner how well knowledge is being actively transferred this week. Target: 100% means all critical skill gaps have a scheduled OJT session.

Unassigned task list with reasons

Tasks that could not be scheduled due to insufficient skilled workers or hour capacity are listed explicitly — creating a visible backlog that the farm manager can address by adjusting worker availability or hiring.

Why It Matters

The farm management problem it solves

Tacit knowledge that can't be written down

Experienced Hawaii coffee farmers know harvest readiness by smell and color — knowledge that doesn't live in any manual. When the senior worker retires or moves, that capability disappears. The planner forces structured co-presence (OJT pairing) that makes tacit knowledge transferable through observation.

Proactive rather than reactive succession

Most farm succession failures are discovered only after the senior leaves. The Monte Carlo stress test simulates departure scenarios before they happen — quantifying exactly which skills would cause operational collapse if a key person were absent this week.

OJT competing with harvest urgency

Without explicit priority weighting, harvest urgency always wins and OJT never happens. By setting ojt urgency_weight=6 (higher than harvest=3) for Monte Carlo-flagged skills, the algorithm forces senior workers to spend time teaching — even in a busy week — protecting the farm's long-term capability.

Downstream input to Yield & Labor Simulator

The stress test's task_failure_probs feed back into the Yield & Labor Simulator's risk_alerts. A farm with critical knowledge concentration in coffee harvesting carries an additional revenue risk — quantified and surfaced to the Farm Owner dashboard alongside weather-driven revenue projections.
